§ 01 / PROBLEM
01The problem

Every robotics team is rebuilding the same broken pipeline.

FILE: pipeline.md
STATUS: obsolete
OWNER: senior-eng
BURN: 6–12 wks
PER SCENE

Capturing a real environment, rebuilding it as a URDF, tuning physics, lighting it for vision policies, and validating sim-to-real transfer takes a senior engineer weeks. Per scene. Then the customer moves a shelf and you start over.

Foundation models like π₀.5 GR00T N1.7 Helix have made the policy layer dramatically more capable. The bottleneck moved upstream — to the data and the environments those models train in.

We built Splice because we were inside that bottleneck. The simulation layer should be ten lines of config, not three months of engineering.

§ 02 / PIPELINE
02How it works

From real to trainable in three steps.

// CAPTURE

Walk through the room.

Walk through your space with an iPhone Pro or any RGB camera rig. Our SDK handles pose estimation, multi-state captures for articulated objects, and lighting reference frames. No marker setup, no specialized hardware.

HardwareiPhone Pro · GoPro · ZED
// RECONSTRUCT

We rebuild it as physics.

Raw video becomes a 3D Gaussian splat scene with auto-discovered articulation, mesh-bound physics, and PBR-decomposed lighting. Articulated objects become URDFs. Static scenes become Isaac Lab and MuJoCo MJX environments.

OutputsURDF · MJCF · USD
// TRAIN

Drop it into your stack.

Compatible with Isaac Lab, MuJoCo MJX, LeRobot, Cosmos, and OpenPI. Render thousands of parallel rollouts on a single H100, with optional synthetic trajectory generation via world-model post-training.

Throughput200+ FPS · 4k env/GPU

§ 05 / OPEN WORK
05Built on the state of the art

We work in the open. Here's what's under the hood.

Splice builds on years of open research into Gaussian splatting, neural physics, and embodied learning. Our reconstruction pipeline implements RoboSimGS-style hybrid splat-mesh representations, our articulation discovery extends ArtGS and URDFormer, and our renderer is a fork of gsplat tuned for batched RL throughput. We're contributors to the LeRobot ecosystem and active in the CoRL and RSS communities.
